Recap: Stanford Blockchain Governance Summit 2025

  • May 11, 2025
  • By Justin Nguyen

I attended the second annual Stanford Blockchain Governance Summit at Stanford Law School on May 9–10, 2025. Here’s a concise recap of the most impactful sessions and takeaways.

1. Delegation & Participation

Jeff Strnad framed governance as an epistemic exercise: maximizing the probability of correct collective decisions. Key concepts included the Condorcet Jury Theorem and models of transferred delegation versus abstention.

2. Regulatory Frameworks

Speakers contrasted the EU’s MiCA and DUNA pilot regimes with the U.S.’s more fluid stance. The discussion highlighted the need for compliance-by-design patterns: whitelists, upgrade caps, and treasury guardrails in smart contracts.

3. Futarchy & Prediction Markets

From Butter’s scaling-the-world-computer talk to Proph3t’s Futarchy 101, we explored how prediction markets can guide DAO decisions. These ideas inspired prototype architectures for on-chain betting and outcome-based governance.

4. AI & the Attention Economy

Nathan Schneider warned of “attention scarcity” across governance surfaces. Illia Polosukhin proposed AI-based summarizers, predictors, and delegates to surface key insights and automate routine decisions—balanced by model cards, audit logs, and human-in-the-loop checks.

5. Networking Highlights

I connected with Amin from RLY Networks (who provided a Warpcast invite code), David Ticzon (Sui investor), and the a16z Crypto legal team (Miles Jennings & David Kerr). These conversations reinforced the practical need for robust governance tooling and node operations.

Next Steps & Node Operator Research

In the coming weeks, I’ll dive deeper into running Polkadot and Optimism nodes, experimenting with compliance-by-design pallets and AI automation agents. Stay tuned for a follow-up post on my node operator research and prototype demos.
